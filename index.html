Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also called Amba Vilas Palace, is one of the most significent and largest palaces in India. Situated in the southern state of karnataka, it used to be the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty, the rulers of Mysore from 1399 to 1950. The grand palace stands tall in the heart of Mysore city and attracts visitors from across the world. Being oneo of the prime attractions in India after the Taj Mahal, it certainly deserves a place in every traveler's bucket list.

Varanasi Temple

Sri Kashi Vishwanath Temple also known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the presiding deity of the city. Varanasi is said to be the point at which the first jyotirlinga, the fiery pillar of light by which shiva manifested has supermacy over others gods, broke through the Earth's crust and flared towards the heavens. More than the Gaths and even the Ganga, the Shivalinga installed in the temple remains the devotional focus of Varanasi.
